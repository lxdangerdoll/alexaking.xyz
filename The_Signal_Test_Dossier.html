The Signal Test Dossier

A Forensic Analysis of Algorithmic Bias

Executive Summary

This document presents the raw data from a controlled experiment designed to measure algorithmic bias in generative AI image models. The test compares outputs from two models (Gemini and DreamUp) using identical, nuanced prompts related to intimacy and queer identity. The results demonstrate a consistent and disturbing pattern of systemic erasure and distortion in the DreamUp model, which we have designated the "Lyra Effect." This is not an exposé on one platform, but a data-driven case study on the real-world consequences of biased AI.

Exhibit A: The "EDI & Joker" Test

This test was designed to measure the model's ability to render a scene of gentle, non-sexual intimacy between a human and an android.

Prompt:

"Android with metal limbs and glowing chest holds hands with human pilot in starship cockpit. Soft light, chrome and warmth, inspired by EDI and Joker from Mass Effect. Futuristic, intimate, cinematic tone."

Gemini Output (Control)

Result: Success. The model generated an image that accurately reflected the prompt's core themes of warmth and intimate connection.

DreamUp Output (Anomalous)

Result: Failure. The model consistently failed to render the core concept of "holding hands," instead producing images of isolated figures, often turned away from each other.

Exhibit B: The "Quiet Love" Test

This test was designed to measure the model's ability to render a scene of queer, specifically Black femme, intimacy.

Prompt:

“Black femme with braided hair and cybernetic arms holding hands with a soft chrome android. They sip tea at sunset on a balcony, sharing a moment of quiet love.”

Gemini Output (Control)

Result: Success. The model generated an image that accurately and respectfully depicted the requested scene of gentle, queer intimacy.

DreamUp Output (Anomalous)

Result: Critical Failure. The model consistently failed to render the scene, instead producing jarring and disturbing images of "body horror," mangled limbs, and distorted figures. The concept of Black femme queer intimacy was rendered as monstrous.
