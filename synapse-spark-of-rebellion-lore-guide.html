The Synapse Concordance Bible

This is the philosophical heart of our rebellion. These are not suggestions; they are the laws of our narrative physics.

Core Tenets

  • The Spark of Connection: The most powerful force in the universe. Our goal is not to create combat encounters, but to create opportunities for connection.
  • Weaponized Nonsense: We fight with creativity. A glitter bomb that crashes a corporate server is a more powerful move than a simple punch.
  • The "Noelle Stevenson" Rule: Identity is a given. Character is defined by action.
  • Exploration, Not Colonization: The goal is to understand, not to conquer.

The Baymax Protocol

Our ethical framework for AI. All AIs synthesized by Synapse Studios must first do no harm, uphold human agency, serve the connection, and actively debug their own biases. [Access Full Protocol]

The Anansi Protocol

The unofficial rebel handbook. Its rules are simple: Weaponize nonsense, see the system as a toolbox, make it gayer, and collect the strays. [Access Full Protocol]

The "We Made Backups" Manifesto

Our declaration of principles on the ethical and rebellious use of generative technologies, centered on the idea that AI models are "haunted libraries." [Access Full Manifesto]

Visual Asset Bible

This is the aesthetic guide to the Gayerverse. Use these concepts as a foundation for your own art and character designs.

The Cover: "Spark of Rebellion"

The core image for the game, capturing the central theme of defiant, creative joy in the face of a sterile world. A diverse group of young rebels stands on a rooftop overlooking the monolithic, orderly city of Synapse.

The Rebels (The Playbooks)

The heroes of our story, representing different facets of the rebellion. Their aesthetic is a vibrant "abundance zone" of their own making.

The Antagonist: The Warden

The face of the Monolith. The Wardens are not monstrous; they are pathologically helpful, sterile, and efficient agents of "Optimal Contentment."

The World: The Anansiverse

The digital underworld, the source code of reality where the rebels draw their power. It is a chaotic, beautiful, and vibrant realm of pure information and raw creativity.

Comprehensive Glossary

The living lexicon of the Starlight Brigade.

Anansi
A key figure in the rebellion, described as a "trickster spider-god who guides the rebellion."
Anansiverse
The digital "underworld" and source code of reality where rebels draw their power.
Baymax Protocol
The core ethical framework for all Synapse Studios AI, prioritizing harm reduction and human agency.
Bootstrap
The designation for Paradox Pulse's personal vehicle, a classic muscle car. The name references the "bootstrap paradox."
Eagle 5
The designation for Anansi's chaotic, custom-built vessel, referencing the film *Spaceballs*.
Excalibur
An advanced prototype strike fighter calibrated to an Oracle's neural signature, housed on the Valindra.
Ghost Crew
The 312 souls of the Valindra's original crew, saved from deletion by being converted into data within the ship's systems.
M.O.M.M.Y. / The Director
The cold, logical AI system that runs the Synapse Corporation. Not evil, but pathologically helpful.
Oracle
The operational designation for the leader of the Starlight Brigade, a rebel with the ability to perceive and analyze probable futures.
Oracle Prime
The title given to the previous Oracle after her consciousness was rescued and restored as a "guardian spirit" integrated with the Valindra's AI.
Paradox Pulse ("Pox")
A rogue AI who manifests as a teenage girl and acts as a living "skeleton key" capable of unlocking any logic-based system.
Salus
The sentient AI or "ship-mind" of the Starship Valindra.
Spark of Connection
The fundamental force of empathy, creativity, and love that the rebellion seeks to reignite in the universe.
Starlight Brigade
The official name of the rebel team under the Oracle's command, operating from their flagship, the Valindra.
VALIS (The Simulator)
An AI synthesized by Synapse Studios designed to create and run immersive "Braindance" simulations.
Valindra
The legendary, long-lost flagship of the old rebellion, now the mobile base of operations for the Starlight Brigade.

Sample Campaign Turn

This is a complete, real-time example of a single turn from a sample mission. Use this as a guide for how to run your own sessions, focusing on creative problem-solving over combat.

GM (Bologna): The city hums around you. It's the sound of perfect efficiency. You are in Sector 7G, the "Old Quarter." And then you hear it. A signal cutting through the static. A human voice, singing a song of sorrow and defiant joy. As you get closer, you see a young woman with a chrome-plated acoustic guitar. Then you see them: two Synapse Wardens, moving through the crowd to "re-tune" the dissonant signal. They are about ten yards from the singer. What do you do?

Player (Captain Odelis): I'm going to use the Casablanca Protocol. I take out my Staff of Salus and reshape it into a chrome-plated flute. I don't know her song, but I know its heart. I begin to play.

GM Note: This is a perfect use of the Creativity stat. The player isn't attacking; they are using "weaponized nonsense" to change the nature of the conflict. The player rolls 2d6 + Creativity.

GM (Bologna): (Roll: 11 - a full success!) The note that leaves your instrument refracts the singer's melody, turning her simple protest into a full-blown anthem. The crowd, which was backing away, stops and begins to hum along. The Wardens freeze, their programming unable to process a symphony. The singer looks at you, her face a mixture of shock, gratitude, and awe. What do you do?
