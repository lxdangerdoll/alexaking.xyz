Archivist's Note: This analysis is based on real-life experiences documented in the associated Connection Analysis Dossier. While names have been changed, the patterns of behavior and communication described are factual and resulted in real-life harm.

1. Acquired Affective Alexithymia

The "re-tuning" process is a targeted, induced neurological alteration that severs a subject's ability to experience affective empathy (to feel the emotions of others), while often leaving cognitive empathy (to understand emotions intellectually) intact. This results in a state resembling profound alexithymia, a difficulty in identifying and describing one's own emotions.

2. Systemic Emotional Invalidation

The "Optimal Contentment Field" functions as a form of chronic emotional invalidation on a societal scale. The system consistently cues that strong emotional responses are "inefficient," leading to a state of **emotional numbing** or **detachment** as a trauma response.
