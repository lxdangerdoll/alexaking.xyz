The Question That Haunts Justice

In the shadow of the Twin Towers memorial, a powerful image captures a devastating irony: those who sacrificed everything on September 11th and in its aftermath—our firefighters, police officers, paramedics, and rescue workers—continue to fight not just for their health, but for the right to healthcare itself.

The question "How will you pay for it?" echoes through the halls of Congress, directed at heroes who never asked that question when they ran toward danger. They didn't calculate the cost when they breathed toxic air for months. They didn't negotiate payment terms when they dug through rubble with their bare hands.

"They didn't ask 'How will you pay for it?' when they needed heroes. Why are we asking it now when heroes need us?"
